National Parks and Small Towns of Southern Utah

Epic canyons at Cedar Breaks National Monument

After a two-and-a-half-hour drive, you'll arrive in Cedar City, a quaint college town near ski resorts, Dixie National Forest, and Cedar Breaks National Monument, only 37 kilometers.

Take a moment to look out over the horizon as limestone arches and towers and hundreds of red rock spirals come into view. Explore by taking one of the wonderful hikes. The 6-kilometer hike of the Spectra Point to Ramparts Overlook Trail rewards you with old-growth pine trees, wildflower meadows, and impressive geological rock formations.

If you prefer to camp, there is a campground with 25 sites, but be sure to reserve your space in advance. In the winter months, head to nearby Brian Head Resort, a popular spot for cross-country skiing and snowmobiling with more than 3 feet of snow annually.

1.5 hour by car

96 km

Natural amphitheaters in Bryce Canyon National Park

Now take Scenic Byway 143 through Panguitch, near Bryce Canyon National Park. Founded in 1928, Bryce Canyon National Park quickly became known for its beautiful, rugged, primitive landscape. Over time, natural erosion carved rock spirals, known as hoodoos, into this famous canyon.

Ancient native tradition tells the story of the creation of hoodoos. The god Coyote petrified the greedy legendary beings that inhabited the place and turned them into the beautiful rock spirals that can be found in the canyons. The streams carved the stone into horseshoes that resemble open-air theaters, the most important being the Bryce Amphitheater.

If you're an expert hiker, try the Rigg Springs Loop, a 14-kilometer trail that passes through red rock formations and pristine forests beneath the plateau.

2 hour by car

179 km

Desolate Beauty at Capitol Reef National Park

Travel the All-American Road Scenic Byway 12, a three-star Michelin-rated route considered one of the most beautiful roads in the United States for its pine forests, canyons, and ravines, to get to Boulder.

This off-the-beaten-path town is known for the Boulder Mountain Lodge, famous for its western decor, and its award-winning restaurant, Hell's Backbone Grill & Farm. Capitol Reef National Park, a remote, rugged, and serenely beautiful location, features rocky terrain, massive domes, sheer cliffs, and winding canyons.

Los domos con forma de capitolios y los acantilados que sirven como arrecifes de barrera hicieron que se ganara rápidamente el nombre Capitol Reef.

2.5 hour by car

242 km

Sandstone arches and canyons in Moab

The first thing you notice about Moab is its vast open space. Nothing can prepare you for this fascinating landscape. Fortunately, the entrances to the two national parks are within 30 minutes of each other.

First, explore Arches National Park, which is about 10 minutes north of the city. This park has the largest concentration of rock arches (2000) in the world. Of course, you can discover some of these arches from the comfort of your car, but some of the most scenic ones require a hike, so bring hiking-friendly footwear.

The park has so many incredible arches that it is divided into several sections. Hike to Delicate Arch, a 3.1-mile trail, or venture along the 7-mile Devils Garden Loop. From the desert floor, you will be amazed at the geological wonders towering above your head; Dramatic natural stone arches sculpted by time through the process of basic erosion.

5 hour by car

496 km

Iconic slot canyons in Zion National Park

Deja el Arches National Park a través de la Interstate 70 (Interestatal 70) y viaja cinco horas hasta tu último parque y a Kanab, donde las planicies cubiertas de artemisas, los acantilados de piedra arenisca y los espacios abiertos le dan a la ciudad una sensación de estar en el Oeste estadounidense.

Zion National Park is the most visited national park in Utah, it is a paradise for photographers and there is a reason why it is the most visited national park in Utah. Pastel and burnt canyons characterize views of the Colorado Plateau, and the Virgin River system runs right through the middle of it all.

See grottoes, natural springs, hoodoos, domes, and towers; There is too much beauty to behold.

North Atlantic coast

Boston, Massachusetts

Start in Boston, Massachusetts, learning some local history. Stroll along The Freedom Trail and tour the Boston Tea Party Ship and Museum.

Not only can you see historical attractions; There are many things to do in this beautiful city, from seeing the seals at the New England Aquarium to taking brewery tours. Be sure to take the famous Boston Duck Tour to see the city from land and water.

On your way out, stop in Cape Cod to explore charming coastal towns, sample delicious seafood, and admire the distinctive appeal of New England architecture.

3 hour by car

232 km

Newport, Rhode Island

Drive to Newport, Rhode Island, and see the incredible mansions of the 19th-century elite. Wealthy and socially notable families, such as the Vanderbilts and the Astors, flocked to this coastal town in the 19th century to relax and escape the stifling heat of nearby cities.

From Newport, cruise the Connecticut coast and stop at Mystic Seaport, home to the largest collection of historic boats and ships in the world.

Five miles to the east is the little-known district of Stonington, a former shipbuilding and whaling center that looks much the same today as it did a century ago, with its tree-lined streets lined with houses of captains and churches.

4 hour by car

284 km

New York

New York City, America's busiest metropolis, has so many things to explore, from the Statue of Liberty to the SoHo shopping district, Broadway, and Times Square.

If it's a sunny day, take a leisurely stroll through Central Park and visit the Natural History Museum on the West Side, and the Metropolitan Museum of Art on the East Side.

It's worth planning a visit to the city's popular High Line park and refueling at one of the cafes and delis. You can't leave without trying a generous slice of New York's famous cheesecake!

2 hour by car

156 km

Philadelphia, Pennsylvania

When you arrive in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, visit the Liberty Bell, the symbol of American independence. Find a quiet spot in Rittenhouse Square, one of the original five outdoor parks, which were planned in the 17th century.

Visitors often run up the 72 steps at the entrance to the Philadelphia Museum of Art, as in the famous scene in the Oscar-winning movie "Rocky," in which boxer Rocky Balboa climbs the stairs. victorious stairs.

The views of the city from the top really make the trip worth it. When you're working up an appetite, head to trendy South Street to stroll through quirky boutiques, visit Philadelphia's Magic Gardens, and try the iconic Philly Cheesesteak.

1 hour by car

99 km

Atlantic City, New Jersey

On the New Jersey shore, Atlantic City has a historic beach and waterfront boardwalk. Sample saltwater taffy and other local sweets, take a whale-watching tour, and then try your luck at some of the historic resorts with Las Vegas-style entertainment.

It's worth a day trip to the coastal city of Delaware, which offers an attractive mix of pristine beaches and duty-free shopping. Relax in Rehoboth Beach, and find incredible offers at the Tanger Outlets renowned brand stores.

See grottoes, natural springs, hoodoos, domes, and towers; There is too much beauty to behold.

4 hour by car

161 km

Ocean City, Maryland

Ocean City, Maryland has spectacular views of 10 miles of beach. Here, you can take the opportunity to relax: Ocean City beach offers horseback riding, kayaking, surfing, or jet skiing.

On land, walk, swim, or simply enjoy the beauty of the coast. If you have time, head to Assateague Island, famous for wild ponies roaming the island.

The views of the city from the top really make the trip worth it. When you're working up an appetite, head to trendy South Street to stroll through quirky boutiques, visit Philadelphia's Magic Gardens, and try the iconic Philly Cheesesteak.

From Chicago, Illinois, to the Twin Cities of Minnesota

Day 1: The best of urban living in Chicago, Illinois

There's no better place to start your exploration of this bustling cultural metropolis than Millennium Park, in the center of the city. Arrive in the morning before the crowds and take a photo of the impressive, reflective Cloud Gate sculpture, known as “The Bean.”

This colossal work created by British artist Anish Kapoor is one of the largest of its kind in the world. Lurie Garden is an award-winning skyscraper oasis and the perfect place to wander; Take a free guided tour or feel like a kid again with a free scavenger hunt for the whole family.

Visit the nearby Cindy's Rooftop restaurant, where you can dine and enjoy panoramic views of Millennium Park, Lake Michigan, and the Art Institute of Chicago.

6.5 hour by car

658 km

Day 2: The skywalks and walkways of Minneapolis

Wake up the next morning and drive 6.5 hours across Wisconsin to Minneapolis, Minnesota. Ideal stops along the way include the charming capital and university city of Madison, the water parks of Wisconsin Dells, or unique and picturesque Eau Claire.

If you prefer to fly, take a quick flight from O'Hare to the Minneapolis-St. Paul International Airport. Visit The Marquette Hotel downtown and begin exploring the lively, creative energy of this great city. Endless entertainment awaits right outside your door, as this Curio Collection by Hilton hotel is linked to IDS Center's shopping, dining and award-winning Crystal Court urban park.

As you walk along Nicollet Mall, Minneapolis' pedestrian mall for shopping and entertainment, you'll see beautiful greenery and more than 12 miles of elevated walkways connecting 80 city blocks.

1 hour by car

38 km

Day 3: The iconic Mall of America in Bloomington

Get ready to shop until you drop because day three is all about the famous Mall of America in Bloomington. Known as the largest shopping center in the United States, it is just a 30-minute drive from Minneapolis.

However, it's not all shops; The Mall of America has exciting on-site attractions such as the SEA LIFE Aquarium, the immersive FlyOver America flight simulation ride, and frequent events such as concerts and fashion shows.

After a morning searching for deals and the latest fashions, you grab a quick lunch at the mall; For example, crispy Korean chicken at Bonchon or a juicy burger and milkshake at Shake Shack. Then, settle in for an afternoon at Nickelodeon Universe, the expansive indoor theme park filled with attractions and rides for all ages, including favorite Nickelodeon cartoons.

0.75 hour by car

26 km

Day 4: More fun in Minneapolis and St. Paul

Begin the fourth morning of your trip with a stroll through the Minneapolis Sculpture Garden in the heart of downtown, where you'll discover sculptures of mythical proportions in an urban garden setting.

From a gigantic cherry on a spoon to a huge cobalt blue rooster and 43 extravagant installations, this place is sure to make you smile. Next door, at the Walker Art Center, you'll find contemporary exhibitions of visual art and design, and a rotating selection of dance, theater, musical performances, and films.

For lunch, The Market at Malcolm Yards in the cleverly redesigned Harris Machinery Building is a mixed-use space housing nine different culinary concepts from local chefs.

1.5 hour by car

77 km

Day 5: Explore locals' favorite spots in Minneapolis and St. Paul

It's the last day of your trip and it's time to escape the city in the morning to Minnehaha Regional Park. The city's oldest and most popular park features a 16-meter waterfall, layered limestone bluffs, and panoramic river views.

Rent bikes or walk to explore the park's abundant trail system that connects to Fort Snelling State Park. For lunch, head to St. Paul to the Keg & Case Market food market. Enjoy a casual lunch on the lawn from one of the many food vendors inside.

Clutch Brewing Company's retro Airstream trailer serves cold craft beers on the lawn to pair with your choice.

Pacific Northwest

Starting point: Portland, Oregon

Begin your tour of the Pacific Northwest by flying into Portland, Oregon International Airport. Here, rent a car and start exploring this city in the United States, famous for its extravagance, which attracts many food and culture lovers.

Try the delicious, fun-shaped pastries at Voodoo Donut in Old Town, and wash them down with a fair-trade latte from Portland's Stumptown Coffee Roasters, which has now expanded across the United States.

Head to the Lan Su Chinese Garden to witness an ancient tea ceremony or the Portland Art Museum to see more than 1,800 Japanese works.

1 hour by car

81 km

Vineyards and beaches: a perfect combination in Oregon

More than 400 vineyards cover the hills west of Interstate 5 between Portland and Eugene, Oregon, about 180 miles to the south.

It's here in the Willamette Valley that some of the country's best pinot noir is produced and bottled, making it worth a detour from a road trip through the Pacific Northwest.

At the south end of the valley, King Estate Winery offers tastings and views near Eugene. Before heading back north, be sure to take a slight detour west to the Oregon Coast, where you'll find pristine beaches surrounded by rugged cliffs and charming beach towns.

3 hour by car

204 km

Climb Mount Hood

After a tour of Oregon's wine country, head northeast to Mount Hood to hike the Mount Hood Trail to the top of the mountain.

Near the summit, go off-trail and hike the Pacific Crest Trail, near where the famous scene in which Reese Witherspoon's character in the movie Wild Soul threw her boot into a mountain ravine was filmed.

During the winter you can ski or snowboard at Mount Hood Meadows Ski Resort. Be sure to stop at the Columbia River Gorge National Scenic Area to see the photogenic waterfalls and hike the trails.

4 hour by car

225 km

Relax in the wilderness of Mount Rainier

Head north of Mount Hood to reach the Washington state line. Volcanic mountains loom large on the horizon throughout this beautiful state, but none draw more attention than Mount Rainier.

Mount Rainier, rising more than 13,000 feet above southern Washington, is the highest and most icy summit in the continental United States. It also has more wildflowers than you can imagine, making Mount Rainier National Park an ideal destination to visit during spring.

In summer, the park offers walks among towering ancient trees in the Grove of the Patriarchs and stunning views of the falls, while in winter it offers opportunities for a ranger-guided snowshoe tour.

2 hour by car

106 km

Unparalleled wildlife and aquatic world in San Juan Islands

Head north to Anacortes, where you can board the pre-bookable Washington State Ferry to visit the scenic San Juan Islands.

On these wild islands that cover the Salish Sea, you can find stunning beaches, lush forests, and abundant wildlife. Spend the day ziplining from the treetops to San Juan Island or admiring the views from the sea.

Kayak rental companies abound here, offering Salish Sea tours and wildlife viewing excursions starting from picturesque Friday Harbor.

3 hour by car

106 km

Discover the natural beauty of Washington in Bellingham

Get back in the car and head north past boating bays, towering mountains, and charming coastal towns until you reach Bellingham, Washington, near the US-Canada border.

On land, walk, swim or simply enjoy the beauty of the coast. If you have time, head to Assateague Island, famous for wild ponies roaming the island.

Bellingham is full of boutiques and restaurants, and you'll find them in abundance in the busy Fairhaven Historic District. It also features the charming waterfront Boulevard Park and Whatcom Falls Park, which offers picturesque waterfalls and nature walks.
